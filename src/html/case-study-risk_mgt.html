Overview

We received client feedback for our Clinical Trial Management System (CTMS) within our Clinical Domain, urging us to upgrade our existing risk management features. The objective was to align with the regulatory norms stipulated by the ICH E6.

  • Understanding ICH E6 - These guidelines were formulated to enhance the safety of subjects participating in trials and to ensure the integrity of data generated.

  • Transformation - To address this, our platform team embarked on developing this feature as the inaugural reusable micro-services platform component.

Goal

To cultivate a scalable modern platform capable of identifying, administering, and reporting study-related risks in compliance with obligatory ICH E6 regulations, while also enriching the user experience within a clinical trial.

Process

Discovery

The exploration phase entailed various methods to comprehend the current needs and obstacles faced by our users. A few of these include:

  • Expert Consultation - Conducted a sequence of dialogues with Study Managers and Department Heads.

  • User Feedback - Gathered persona insights from Marketing, consulted with Product Management on product roadmaps, and analyzed customer support logs.

  • Current State Analysis - Determined the existing status of our services and conducted a comprehensive heuristic evaluation.

  • Collaboration with Services Team - Joined forces with our services team to pinpoint issues with the current system and to identify requests.

Design System

We leveraged a design system to ensure uniformity across all applications within our LifeSphere suite. This system served as a single source of truth and comprehensive guide, promoting consistent visual language and user interaction patterns, thereby enhancing usability.

In addition, we prioritized accessibility during our design phase. We used a dedicated tool within Figma to regularly check and validate accessibility standards, ensuring our designs were inclusive and user-friendly for all users, regardless of their abilities or circumstances.

Feedback

Our user testing phase helped us garner valuable insights into the needs and obstacles of our users. Some key feedback included:

  • Categorizing Risks - Domain Managers emphasized the importance of concentrating on risks associated with their specific domain. Simultaneously, Study Managers expressed the need to view all risks across all domains.

  • Conciseness - To avoid overly lengthy descriptions, users suggested implementing a character limit in the risk description field.

  • Current State Analysis - An established baseline outlining the current state was required, along with a comprehensive heuristic evaluation.

  • Need for Guidance - Users indicated a need for a tooltip explaining the risk score calculation, as well as guidance on drafting a risk.

Projected Results & Metrics

Our aim is to demonstrate the value of our enhanced Risk Management module by illustrating how it aids our clients in saving both time and money.

  • Increase Usage - We plan to implement Pendo to monitor and graphically represent the increase in usage. As a part of this initiative, we are running a pilot project with a top client.

  • Boosting Adoption - By December 2023, we forecast the addition of two new customers, who will recognize this upgraded feature as a key motivation to adopt CTMS.

  • Reduce Task Time - The time taken to input a new risk has been cut in half, with RACT now requiring 19 clicks and MCC only needing 8.

Furthermore, our Sales team is eager to market this new feature, confident that it will boost adoption rates. Concurrently, our Marketing team is introducing new branding, featuring screenshots and live demos from this project as part of the launch of our revamped website.
