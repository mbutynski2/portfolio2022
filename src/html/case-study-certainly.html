Certainly Insurance

Online Insurance Rebrand

  • Role: Senior UX Designer
  • Timeframe:2 months
  • Team: 5UX, PM, MK, 5SE
  • Company:Liberty Mutual

Overview

Liberty Mutual's innovation team would like to rebrand their online insurance platform to better meet the needs of their target persona. This product was originally designed to offer only renters insurance for an older demographic. Once the product began to scale for auto and small business insurance, Liberty Mutual needed to rethink the user experience based on research to introduce a new younger persona they call Stephanie+.

Goal

Deliver user-led designs to better meet the needs of Stephanie+ for now and in the future.

Process

Laying the Foundation

As a dedicated design team, we utilized many methods to influence our designs. Here are a few:

  • Heuristic Evaluation - Leaned on UX principles and industry best practices.

  • Collaboration - Marketing download of Stephanie+; Product Management roadmaps; Customer support logs.

  • Competitors - Researched to perform task analysis; Discover ways to differentiate.

  • User Testing - Ran moderated and unmoderated testing using Userzoom on current design.

Current State Feedback was Key

Since the site was live, we had a great opportunity to gather user feedback quickly. Here is some of the feedback:

  • Color - Muted and uninviting; Colors compete for hierarchy; Red has a negative connotation.

  • Iconongraphy - Muted blue looks 'out of focus'; Red looks alarmist; Every icon looks like an alert or error instead of looking instructive.

  • Type - Outdated, rigged, and not modern / tech; Not versatile and limited weights/styles within the font group; Struggles with legibility.

  • Photography - Faded wash treatment is limiting; The wash takes away vibrance and relatability; The need of overexposed and light background imagery is retructive.

Design is Messy

We explored many different solutions and met as a team to collaborate to define the winning design.

My favorite Slide

This is such a great representation of how font choice can express so much.

I offered the client a closer experience by displaying the non-coded prototype on an actual tablet. By using the Figma mirroring app, anyone I share the prototype with could mirror the experience directly on the tablet.

Results

We presented the app and they were delighted by the interface. We increased job satisfaction and flight efficiencies by enabling flight attendants to perform their flight checklists more quickly (IPad entry versus paper logs). We improved productivity of overall flight operations with reduction of manual data entry. We established growth potential of user experience with a scalable, tablet enriched environment for user experience (features and functions) expansion over time.
