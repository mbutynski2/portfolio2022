Overview

Liberty Mutual's innovation team met with customers to confirm the need for rebranding the Certainly online insurance platform. Based on user insights, the UX team was tasked to better meet the needs of our target persona. This product was originally designed to offer only renters insurance for an older demographic. Once the product began to scale for auto and small business insurance, Liberty Mutual needed to modernize the user experience to entice a new younger persona they call Stephanie+.

Goal

Deliver user-led designs to better meet the needs of Stephanie+ for now and in the future.

Process

Laying the Foundation

As a dedicated UX design team, we utilized many methods to influence our designs. Here are a few:

  • Heuristic Evaluation - Leaned on UX principles and industry best practices.

  • Collaboration - Received persona understanding from Marketing; Met with Product Management on roadmaps; Utilized customer support logs.

  • Competitors - Performed task analysis to discover ways to differentiate.

  • User Testing - Ran moderated and unmoderated testing using Userzoom on current design.

Current State Feedback was Key

Since the site was live, we had a great opportunity to gather user feedback quickly. We interviewed 10 candidates matching the desired persona and logged the feedback. Here are some highlights:

  • Color - Muted and uninviting; Colors compete for hierarchy; Red has a negative connotation.

  • Iconography - Muted blue looks 'out of focus'; Red looks alarmist; Every icon looks like an alert or error instead of looking instructive.

  • Type - Outdated, rigged, and not modern / tech; Not versatile and limited weights/styles within the font group; Struggles with legibility.

  • Photography - Faded wash treatment is limiting; The wash takes away vibrance and relatability; The need of overexposed and light background imagery is restrictive.

Empowered by Feedback, Exploration Began

As designers, we are natural born creative problem solvers. Each of us got a chance to get into 'headphone mode' and design solutions. We embraced the 'all windows no walls' concept and trusted one another to invite criticism through a 2 week design sprint.

Most valuable deliverable

Our most important deliverable was a design system clearly defined for all designers and developers to lean on moving forward. We quickly began partnering with development in building out components using Storybook.

Results

This project was a testament to utilizing customer feedback as the bedrock for innovative design. I learned to dig deep and value trust in supporting the creative genius of a high performance design team. We empowered the development team with reusable assets to increase efficiency and reduce time to deliver our MVP. After this project I asked myself, was this collaboration a success? ...Certainly.
