Approval Central

Tasks management made easy

  • Role: UX Design Lead
  • Timeframe:4 Weeks
  • Team: UX, SA, SM, BA, 5SE
  • Company:DXC

Overview

DXC leadership would like a system that consolidates many types of digital management tasks into a central view for managers to take action. My role as the Lead UX Designer is to lead discovery, design, and assist the development team with code styling.

Goal

Develop a responsive working application that performs key functionality in 4 weeks. We're looking for this product to decrease manager delays and allow for a more streamlined tool to avoid errors.

Process

Understanding the problem

Mergers & acquisitions have made for a disparity of systems. The current state for DXC leadership requires navigating among 30+ different application across 7 different business functions to perform and to keep track of requests.

Each system has unique loginâ€™s, security requirements, notification settings, and processes. This has caused endless delays and confusion across all business functions.

Below is a user flow illustrating the current state scenario of an employee requesting a headset from their manager.

Took initiative to talk to users

In the initial kickoff meeting with the stakeholders, I requested access to a few users to interview. I collaborated with the business analyst on the team to develop and facilitate a test plan. Together we formalize interview questions, scheduled and ran interviews, systhesized the data, and presented the findings to the team.

Lead team collaboration

Armed with user feedback, I began to solution and invited the team to collaborate using Miro. This tool allowed for a focused discussion around a suggested user flow based on user feedback. We used Miro to collaborate and record decision making. I led this discovery session and iterated in real time. We formalized the path forward which set the team on a guided path to success.

Prototype

Here is the final design approved by the stakeholders and provided to development using Figma. The user journey shown is Manager Katie who wants to take action on a few procurement requests.

Supporting Artifacts

Here are some of the low fidelity exploration and styleguide materials created to help communicate effectively with stakeholders and development.

Results

The POC was approved and the MVP deployed! We reduced manager headaches from more than 15 minutes initially to less than 5 minutes to resolve a request. This resulted in a decrease of time to task by at least 10 minutes per request. We also improved our manager procurement efficency rating from 36% unsatisfactory to 92% outstanding.
