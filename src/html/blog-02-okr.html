OKR's

Measuring What Matters

A simple method on how to take your OKR's to OMG's

For all product design managers, a great tool to have in your toolbox is the ability to create great OKR's. When I was first introduced to OKR’s by John Doerr in his book “Measure What Matters”, I felt that this was the missing manual on how to be a successful manager. By leaning on ORK's, managers are free to empower their employees in leading their destiny. Utilizing great OKR's allows managers to help guide, mentor and coach thier creatives into achieving their goals.

Breaking down the basics

OKR’s are performance indicators. They help us focus on important issues. An objective is what you want to accomplish. Example: Improve voice of customer. Key results are how you will accomplish it. Example: Talk to 5 customers about our roadmap.

The benefits

By adopting the method of establishing and tracking OKR’s you allow teams to move with agility and drive the needs of their team.

“The one thing an OKR system should provide par excellence is focus.”

Andy Grove
Father of ORK's

How to create

We will ____(objective) as measured by _____(these key results).

Key results determine whether an objective has succeeded or failed. The golden rule of writing key results is that any reasonable person would agree that completing all the key results would guarantee completion of the objective. Be very clear when defining key results. Make sure they are quantifiable and whenever possible use metrics instead of binary results.

Formula for success

When crafting your great OKR', be sure to take into account these few things:

Overall company mission - step back and take a birds eye view to make sure your vision is in alignment with the company mission.

Your org - next take a look at your product department strategy, goals, and roadmap to ensure consistency.

Your team - finally align with your team to consider how your goals will directly align to your organization and achieve what matters most.

Trust the process

If your team is new to OKR’s, know that it will take a few cycles to set in. Once your team has defined their north star, then everyone’s skills and talents are free to roll up their sleeves and drive our organization forward utilizing great OKR's.

In my view, you can only do one big thing at a time really well, so you better know what the one thing is.

- Brett Kopf, Cofounder of Remind
