The Design Brief

Kick-off Your Next Design Project with 6 Key Questions

I'll keep it brief...

A comprehensive design brief is akin to the backbone of any product design project. Not only does it serve as a guiding compass for the team, but it also ensures everyone is on the same page, minimizing the potential for misunderstandings and misdirection.

Let's consider a hypothetical project: designing a mobile application for an online bookstore. Iâ€™ll walk you through how to answer these six questions in the early stages of your next new design project:

1. Clarity of Purpose: "What are we solving?"

This may seem obvious, yet it is often overlooked. A concise statement of the problem, devoid of requirements or constraints, sets a clear goal for the team. It focuses their efforts on what truly matters and prevents scope creep.

2. Knowing Your Audience: "Who is our target audience?"

Identifying 1-2 key personas and envisaging their use-cases enables designers to create solutions that resonate with the user's needs, preferences, and behaviors. It's about designing 'for' the user, not just 'a' user.

3. Success Indicators: "What are the success measurements?"

It is crucial to outline how the project's success will be measured and how it aligns with the company's vision. This helps in quantifying the project's impact and ensuring it contributes to the broader organizational objectives.

4. Constraints and Limitations: "What are the design constraints?"

By identifying constraints such as technology and budget upfront, the team can work within defined parameters, ensuring more accurate planning and execution.

5. Team Dynamics: "Who's who?"

Identifying key team members and their roles fosters collaboration and clarifies responsibilities. It enables efficient communication, promoting a cohesive and effective team.

Key Milestones: "What are the important milestones?"

Outlining milestones like launch dates, demos, and decisions, provides a roadmap for the project. It enables the team to manage their time effectively and ensures critical deadlines are met.

Final thoughts

In conclusion, a well-constructed design brief offers clarity and direction. It helps in aligning the team's efforts with the project's objectives, ultimately leading to better results. So, for successful project execution, start with an insightful design brief.
