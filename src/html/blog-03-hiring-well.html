Hiring Well

Leaning into the great recession

Today, product designers are in demand.

This putting creatives in the drivers seat to explore job options of interest that better align to their values. In my design leadership role, I was having challenges keeping up with the pace the market demands while performing my duties as Associate Director of Product Design at ArisGlobal.

I didn’t want to regret making an offer due to quick decisioning and souling relying on my gut, so I created a simple solution to speed the early evaluation process. This ‘secret sauce’ allows for more time focused on the right applicants with the goal of mutual fit.

Here's my go-to-guide:

Step 1 - Soul searching

Take a moment and evaluate the needs of your team. Identify both soft skills and technical skills required to be successful in the role. What skills are your team lacking? Knowing the org, what types of characteristics are required to be successful? Define what you are looking for and review it with your in-house recruiter. Shape the posting and screener questions to seek out these candidates.

Step 2 - Ally in your recruiter

Your recruiter is going to gather first-hand knowledge of the applicants expectations. They are your direct link to the word on the street. Together, build an interview process plan defined by team needs and feedback from your recruiter interviews. Schedule a recurring cadence to check and adjust. Agile baby!

Step 3 - Build a checklist

This is truly the secret sauce. A checklist grounds your attentiion to the necessary skills and attributes you are seeking. Once your applicant passes the initial screen, next armed with your handy dandy checklist, you can begin to distill the applicant even further based on accessing the resume, their portfolio, and their LinkedIn profile. I have gotten down to 10 minutes per applicant in this assessment.

Example checklist for a Senior Product Designer

Note:

if you are an applicant and have done your research, mention the phrase “secret sauce”. Wink!

Final thoughts

In the lengthy vetting process of a potential candidate, by being strategic and removing bias, you can quickly distill the right candidates to focus your time. This will allow attention where it is needed most… in finding the right fit for your team to thrive.

In art school at Savannah College of Art and Design, I was at my highest sense of purpose while fine-tuning the muscle of drawing in an environment surrounded by like-minded creative artists. I am fully aware that great art starts with a sketch, so my habit was an easy one to choose.

With my intention set of “I will bring my artist's eye to my work and quickly design user experiences that delight”, I began to draw each day.

Here are the top 5 things I have learned so far:

1. Creative fuel

By stepping away for a short time and laser focusing on sketching an object, I freed my mind to solve problems creatively throughout the day. It can be described as clearing out the cobwebs in your head so when a challenge comes your way, you are clear and quick to solve the problem.

2. Confidence booster

I felt more empowered to translate ideas in my head to the paper. This fine-tuned hand eye coordination was a confidence booster to deliver concepts quickly. I noticed I was able to deliver creative solutions quicker and able to defend my ideas more confidently.

3. Time wasted

With James’s concept of signing up for only 2 minutes a day, I removed the hurdle of not having enough time and also noticed more and more opportunities to get in my two minute drawing time. Example - while waiting in line for my Covid vaccine shot; Example - while stuck in traffic (note: I do not endorse this but I doodled on a receipt in my car at a dead stop. wink)

4. Live by example

This habit fostered my kids to jump on the bandwagon. We have shared special moments doodling together and I stay inspired by their playful representation of life.

5. Mandi the artist - (still smile when I read this :)

I do consider myself a practicing artist with this habit now ingrained in my everyday life. I also have a greater sense of purpose due to fostering my artist identity. Stay tuned to see how my doodles and sketches evolve into paintings!

My advice for those wanting to establish a new habit, make sure it fosters a deeper sense of self. Next, take it slow. Commit to only 2 minutes a day. Each day, you will begin to see opportunities to evolve and grow into the person you want to become.
