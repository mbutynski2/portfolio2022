Hiring Well

Leaning into the great recession

Today, product designers are in demand.

This has put creatives in the driver's seat to explore job options of interest or ones that better align to their values. In my design leadership role, I was having challenges keeping up with the pace of market demands while performing my duties as Associate Director of Product Design at ArisGlobal.

I didn‚Äôt want to regret making an offer due to quick decision making and relying on my gut, so I created a simple solution to speed the early evaluation process. This ‚Äòsecret sauce‚Äô allows for more time focused on the right applicants with the overall goal of mutual fit among the team.

Here's my go-to-guide:

Step 1 - Soul searching

Take a moment and evaluate the needs of your team. Identify both soft skills and technical skills required to be successful in the role. What skills are your team lacking? Knowing the org, what types of characteristics are required to be successful? Define what you are looking for and review it with your in-house recruiter. Shape the job posting and screener questions to seek out these candidates.

Step 2 - Ally in your recruiter

Your recruiter is going to gather first-hand knowledge of the applicants expectations. They are your direct link to the word on the street. Together, build an interview process plan defined by team needs and feedback from your recruiter interviews. Schedule a recurring cadence to check and adjust the process and posting. Agile baby!

Step 3 - Build a checklist

This is truly the secret sauce. A checklist grounds your attention to the necessary skills and attributes you are seeking. Once your applicant passes the initial screen, next armed with your handy dandy checklist, you can begin to distill the applicant even further based on accessing the resume, portfolio, and LinkedIn profile. I have gotten down to 10 minutes per applicant in this assessment.
